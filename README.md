# Amazon India Sales 2025 – Data Analysis & Dashboard
Interactive dashboard creation - using Tableau

> **Data Objective** ( Purpose of the data ) :

The Amazon Diwali India Sales Dataset (2025) is a synthetic dataset containing 15,000 e-commerce transactions designed to simulate real-world sales on Amazon India. It captures customer purchasing behavior, product details, payment preferences, and feedback patterns across various product categories throughout the year 2025.

**Dataset Overview (Amazon Sales – 2025, India)**
- Total Orders                            : 15,000
- Total Sales (Gross)                     : ₹1.12 Billion
- Net Sales (after returns & adjustments) : ₹981.8 Million
- Product Categories                      : Electronics, Beauty, Books, Clothing, Home & Kitchen

- Geography                               : Pan-India (all states)
- Payment Methods                         : UPI, Credit Card, Debit Card, Cash on Delivery
- Customer Feedback                       : Ratings & review text included

## **Project Insights & Dataset Overview** 

**Sales & Revenue Performance**
- Beauty (₹199.8M) and Electronics (₹198.6M) are the top-performing categories, contributing the highest net revenue.
- Revenue is well distributed across categories, reducing dependency on a single product line.
- Net sales dropped ~12% from gross sales, mainly due to returns and adjustments.

**Returns & Delivery Analysis**
- Return Rate: 12.1% (moderate but impactful on revenue)
- Returned orders significantly reduce profitability, especially in high-value products.
- Improving delivery accuracy and product descriptions can directly boost net sales.
  
![DSBD](https://github.com/Pavankumar-Bishnu/Tableau_Project_File_2/blob/main/DSBD.png) 

**Customer Ratings & Satisfaction**
- Average Rating                          :  3.04 / 5
- Indicates average customer satisfaction.
- Products with lower ratings correlate with higher return volumes → opportunity for quality improvement.

**Payment Method Trends**
- Payment usage is almost evenly split
  - Cash on Delivery                      : 25.5%
  - Credit Card                           : 25.3%
  - Debit Card                            : 24.8%
  - UPI                                   : 24.3%
- Digital payments (UPI + Cards) account for ~75%, showing strong digital adoption in India.

**Geographic Performance**
- Top Revenue-Generating States:
- Sikkim
- Rajasthan
- Meghalaya
- Bihar
- Chhattisgarh
- Indicates strong sales even in Tier-2 and Tier-3 regions, not just metro cities.

**Final Conclusion**

- This dataset shows that Amazon India in 2025 has a strong and diversified sales ecosystem, driven by:
   - Balanced category performance
   - Wide geographic reach
   - Strong digital payment adoption
- However, profitability can be significantly improved by:
  - Reducing product returns
  - Improving customer satisfaction (ratings)
  - Strengthening logistics and product quality
